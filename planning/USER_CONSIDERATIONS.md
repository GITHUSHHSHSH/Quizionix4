# Quizionix User Considerations (Grade 11–12 STEM, OLFU)



## Audience Snapshot
- Students User: Grade 11–12 STEM curriculum (100 respondents)
- Institution: Our Lady of Fatima University (OLFU)
- Context: Research project output; website-based 2D quiz RPG

## Primary User Goals
- Practice STEM topics with immediate feedback
- Track improvement over time without pressure
- Complete short sessions that fit between classes or study blocks

## User Context & Constraints
- Likely mixed device access (phones, laptops, school PCs)
- Variable internet quality; must load fast and work on low-end devices
- Sessions may be short (5–10 minutes) due to schedules
- Students may be using school accounts; privacy is critical

## Motivation & Engagement
- Students respond to clear progress indicators (levels, XP, mastery)
- Competition can help, but avoid public shaming or forced leaderboards
- A light RPG framing can increase engagement without needing full story

## Learning Experience Priorities
- Clear alignment between questions and STEM curriculum topics
- Balanced difficulty progression; avoid abrupt spikes
- Explanations for wrong answers to encourage retry and learning
- Recognize mastery per topic, not just overall score

## Accessibility & Usability
- High-contrast UI options and legible typography
- Keyboard-friendly navigation for school PCs
- Minimal steps from login to first quiz
- Consistent, predictable navigation patterns

## Trust, Safety, and Data Ethics
- Collect only minimal data required for research metrics
- Use anonymized or pseudonymous identifiers for reporting
- Provide clear consent language for data use in research outputs

## Research Alignment
Proposed research questions (drafts):
- Does RPG-style progression improve quiz completion rates for STEM students?
- Do short-session quizzes increase retention and return rate?

## Metrics to track:
- Completion rate per session
- Accuracy by topic and difficulty
- Retry rate after incorrect answers
- Time-on-task per session
- Return rate after 1 and 7 days

## Success Criteria (Draft)
- Students can start a quiz within 2 minutes after login
- Average session completion rate >= 70%
- Positive student feedback on clarity and motivation

## Open Items for the Team
- List of priority STEM topics per grade level
- Question bank sourcing and validation process
- Data storage method and retention policy
